# Exercise Ten — Substack as a Brand Platform, Owned

**Course:** INFO 7375 Branding & AI · backs **Assignment 10** (Show Your Work — Substack as a Brand Platform)
**What you'll build:** a home you own — a brand-aligned Substack publication, a brand-consistency audit across your three platforms, one 600–800 word thought-leadership article with a custom visual, a promotion post, and a 300-word strategic reflection — by **conducting Ogilvy, BRANDY, and Nina** while **you own the voice, the useful idea, and the strategy.**
**Time:** one session for the setup, the audit, and the article draft; publish + promote is the homework after.
**You need:** your **Assignment 9** story arcs + expanded narrative (this is where that work deploys), your **A7/A8** brand system (palette, type, logo, headers), your **`brand/resume.json`** (truth source), the `ogilvy` / `brandy` / `nina` skills, and a free Substack account.

The principle this exercise runs on: **the best self-promotion never feels like self-promotion — because it isn't.** A10 is the move from a *borrowed* platform (the A9 LinkedIn article) to one you *own*. The fluency trap here has a name the rubric gives you directly: the bottom-quartile article *"reads like an assignment, not a real publication."* Its twin is the product announcement dressed as an essay — the piece that mentions your tool but gives the reader nothing they can use. The discipline that closes both: **the article earns attention by being genuinely useful and true, and the tool earns its mention by adding value to the reader's idea — not the other way around.** Self-promotion that works is a demonstration of your thinking, and thinking can't be faked into fluency.

There's a second discipline stacked on top: **brand consistency is the trace audit at the platform level.** Your Substack must look like it belongs to the same family as your site and LinkedIn — not identical, clearly related. That's the same "every choice traces to the A7 system" rule you've run since Exercise 7, now audited *across* properties.

The division of labor, one more time, is the lesson — and A10 states it: *Claude can help you structure your argument and sharpen your draft; just make the final voice yours.* **Ogilvy and Nina do the Tier-1 work** — the headline options, the hook, the publication copy, the scannable structure. **BRANDY does the audit** — observe the touchpoints, label the shared evidence, build the matrix. **You do the irreducible work** — the useful idea at the article's core, the voice, and the strategic choices the reflection has to prove you made on purpose.

## Move 0 — Bring forward A9 and your brand system

Pick your path (A: personal brand · B: the Madison tool startup). Pull your **strongest A9 arc + expanded narrative** — A10 is explicitly where that storytelling deploys — and open your A7/A8 brand assets (palette, type, logo, the headers you already built). The publication isn't a fresh start; it's the home those pieces have been pointing at.

## Move 1 — Launch the publication (Part 1, 10 pts)

```
/tagline     # Ogilvy: one sharp sentence — what the reader gets, not what you are
/blurb web   # Ogilvy: the 150-200 word About page (open with the value, not your name)
```

Apply your **A7/A8 visual assets** to the Substack header/logo and set the custom URL (`yourname.substack.com`). Keep Nina's `/n6` voice rules in view — this is the voice that will *develop over time*, so set it deliberately now. The About page should pass the same IS-NOT test as every other piece: if it sounds like a generated bio, rewrite it with a true, specific line.

## Move 2 — Brand consistency audit (Part 1, 10 pts) — this is a BRANDY job

```
brandy       # observe the three touchpoints, label every shared element
onepage      # the one-page written explanation
```

Feed BRANDY your three headers — **Substack / LinkedIn / Website hero** — and let it do what it's built for: observe each touchpoint, **label the evidence** (the palette hexes, the type, the logo treatment, the voice), and produce the **cohesion matrix**. Then write the 3–5 sentence explanation of *why* the three feel related (same system, deliberate variation) — grounded in the labeled observations, not vibes. Screenshot the three; assemble the one-page PDF. This is the trace audit across platforms, and BRANDY is the purpose-built instrument for it.

## Move 3 — The article (Part 2, 50 pts)

A 600–800 word piece that does three things **at once**: establishes you as credible, introduces the tool *with* value, and **leaves the reader something genuinely useful** — a framework, an insight, a perspective they didn't have. Drive Ogilvy:

```
/hook         # the first two sentences must earn the third
/credibility  # real proof only — every stat is [VERIFY — human], never an unconfirmed number
/benefit      # the tool, framed as what it lets the reader DO — woven in, not bolted on
/seo          # findable and indexed (the whole point of an owned platform)
/cta          # one clear ask: subscribe / try the tool / connect / comment
/edit         # 600-800 words, scannable: headline, subheads, short paragraphs
```

Build the spine from your strongest A9 arc. **Publish it live on Substack** — a draft is not a deliverable.

**Your gate — the two tests this article lives or dies on:**
- **The usefulness test:** finish the article, then ask *what does the reader now have that they didn't?* If the answer is "they know my tool exists," it's a product announcement — go back and give them the framework.
- **The integration test:** delete the tool mention. If the article still stands, the tool was bolted on — rework it so the tool is the natural extension of the useful idea, not an interruption of it.

## Move 4 — The custom visual (Part 2, within Article)

One on-brand visual that **enhances the content** — a data visualization, a process diagram, a branded graphic, or an annotated tool screenshot:

- **Diagram / data viz** → the `cajal` skill (`/scan` the draft, `/show` to render a colorblind-safe SVG in your A7 palette).
- **Branded graphic** → Nina `/n7` (visual world) + `/n9` (the generation prompt) → Canva / Firefly.

It carries your A7 system, same as every visual since Exercise 7.

## Move 5 — Promotion post (Part 2, 10 pts)

```
/linkedin    # Ogilvy: 2-4 sentences that give value or tease the key insight — never "I wrote a thing"
```

Post the caption with the article link and a screenshot/graphic from the piece as the image. Capture the post (with any engagement) for the PDF.

## Move 6 — The 300-word reflection (Part 3, 20 pts) — yours alone

This is the part no tool writes for you, because it's the evidence you thought strategically. In 300 words: the choices you made (angle, tone, structure) **and why**; how this positions you differently from others in your field; what you'd do differently; and — one sentence — what the *next* piece would be about. Ogilvy `/edit` can tighten the prose; the strategy is the human deliverable, and it's worth a fifth of the grade because it's where mechanical execution and real thinking become distinguishable.

## Move 7 — Assemble + log

Single Canvas PDF: live **publication link**, live **article link**, the **brand-consistency audit** (screenshots + explanation), the **LinkedIn post** screenshot, and the **300-word reflection**. Then one `logs/RUN_LOG.md` entry: your path, your publication name + tagline, your live URLs, the one useful idea your article hands the reader, and the labeled observation from the BRANDY audit you're most confident ties the three platforms together.

The whole 20 turns on the same axis the course has taught all term: useful-and-true beats polished-and-empty.

## What can go wrong

| Symptom | What it means | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| It reads like an assignment, not a publication | written for a professor, not an audience | write for the reader who never promised you attention — lead with the useful idea, not the requirement |
| It's a product announcement | the tool is the subject, not the value | run the usefulness test — the reader must leave with a framework; the tool serves *their* idea |
| The tool mention is bolted on | integration failed the delete test | rework so the tool is the natural extension of the insight, not an interruption |
| The three headers don't feel related | brand inconsistency across platforms | run BRANDY — label the shared palette/type/logo/voice; add the missing through-line |
| A stat in the article is unverified | provenance gap on your own claims | `[VERIFY — human]` every number; only publish figures you can source |
| "Published" is a draft / a PDF | not live | the live Substack URL is the deliverable; a draft scores zero on the article |
| The reflection just describes what you did | execution narrated, not strategy named | name the *why* behind each choice and how it positions you — that's the 20 points |
